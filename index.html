J-pop duo Yoasabi say their pandemic era success feels surreal

Active from 2019

Yoasobi is a Japanese pop duo formed in 2019 by Sony Music Entertainment Japan.

Yoasobi/Genres

J-pop Vocaloid Utaite

"Yoru ni Kakeru"

Both members of Yoasobi had active music careers before the duo formation. Ayase, a singer-songwriter, producer, musician, and then-vocalist of rock band Davinci (2012–2020),[1] began using Vocaloid software Hatsune Miku to produce music and uploaded his first song, "Sentensei Assault Girl", on the video-sharing website Niconico in 2018 and gained popularity with "Last Resort" a year later.

Hajimete no, "Idol"

Yoasobi collaborated with four Naoki Prize-winning novelists to perform four songs based on their short stories under the theme of "a story to read when you do [something] for the first time". It contains Rio Shimamoto's Watashi Dake no Shoyūsha, Mizuki Tsujimura's Yūrei, Miyuki Miyabe's Iro Chigai no Trump, and Eto Mori's Hikari no Tane.
